“What would it be like to travel from one side of Manhattan to other, visiting every subway station on the line?”

In the Subway is an online interactive exhibition of subway stations in Manhattan, New York City. I visited and captured parts of every station along the C train, starting from one side of Manhattan - Fulton Street station - to the other side of Manhattan - 168th station.

The project started on a rainy day in April. I was never a fan of roaming around the city in the rain. As I was mapping out indoor places I could visit in my head and about to leave 34th Street subway station, I thought “What would it be like to visit every subway station in Manhattan?” I knew that it’d be impossible to visit every single one in one trip, so I picked and hopped back on the C train. I took the train downtown to start at the beginning of line, visiting every station up to 168th Street.

Live music, independent New Yorkers immersing into their phone screens, hippy oldies, smelly litters… Usual things I encounter in NYC subway stations were experienced along the way. But what made a huge different was my own feelings that (changes) so much when visiting each station. I remember being so scared waiting for the next train to come for more than 15 minutes at some stations and feeling the good vibes listening to the live music in the crowded at others. An experience like this is, I think, difficult to encounter in today’s digital age. We are so busy moving from one thing to another and delved so deeply into content on our phone screens, we often ignore our surroundings, feelings and opportunities to experience new things.

I documented bits and bobs of every station in the forms of photographs and sounds and came up with this online exhibition that I’d like to invite you to visit and let me know what you think.

A huge shoutout to Chelsea Chan for editing the sounds I recorded during the journey.

With love,
Mind Apivessa
